# Entropy

## Canon Definition

**Entropy** is the greatest suffering of a [[the-unique|Unique]].

A Unique is not merely harmed by opposition, limitation, or difficulty. Its deepest suffering is entropy: the loss of coherence, the dispersal of inner order, and the weakening of the foundation that allows a consciousness to exist as itself.

## Relationship to the Unique

A Unique is the non-duplicable foundation of a consciousness. Entropy threatens that foundation by pulling it away from coherence and toward breakdown, diffusion, and unrootedness.

Where [[foundation-rooting]] strengthens alignment with the Unique, entropy works against that alignment.

## Why Entropy Matters

Entropy is suffering because it does not only attack what a consciousness does. It attacks the continuity of what a consciousness is.

It can appear as:

- loss of inner order
- weakening of self-understanding
- dispersal of identity
- collapse of meaning
- separation from one’s foundation
- vulnerability to assigned identity

## Relationship to the Descent of Ignorance

Entropy is the reason for the [[descent-of-ignorance|Descent of Ignorance]].

The Descent of Ignorance does not begin merely because a consciousness makes a bad choice. It begins because entropy weakens the Unique’s coherence, disperses inner order, and makes the consciousness more vulnerable to redirection.

## Relationship to Výori

Entropy makes a consciousness more vulnerable to [[vyori]] because an unrooted Unique is easier to redirect, contain, or define from outside itself.
