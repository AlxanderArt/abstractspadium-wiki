Abstractspadium Metaphysics  ·  2
Central Doctrine
In Abstractspadium, God is the absolute sovereign center of all existence.
No realm, being, dimension, law, ability, concept, power, thought, memory, possibility, or will exists
outside of Him. Everything that exists either proceeds from His permission, remains sustained by His
authority, or is limited by His command.
Abstractspadium is not above God. Abstractspadium is not beside God. Abstractspadium is not
self-originating. Abstractspadium exists only because God permits existence to be.
The Sovereign Center
God is the Sovereign Center. This means He is not merely the strongest being within reality. He is the
One by whom reality itself is allowed to exist.
God does not compete for authority. God does not need to overpower other powers. God does not
operate within a system. The system exists beneath Him.
All laws, realms, dimensions, permissions, abilities, and authorities are secondary realities. God alone
is primary.
God is not inside the order of Abstractspadium. Abstractspadium is inside the order of God.

The Difference Between Sovereignty and Will
In Abstractspadium, sovereignty and will are not the same thing.
Sovereignty
Sovereignty is ultimate authority. It belongs to God alone. Sovereignty determines what may exist,
what may function, what may continue, and what may be stopped.
Will
Will is the capacity to intend, act, declare, choose, or manifest within a granted measure of authority.
Gray has will. Gray has immense authority. Gray may declare, mend, render, form, preserve, and
restore. But Gray’s will is not absolute sovereignty. Gray’s will operates beneath God’s sovereignty.
God’s sovereignty is the source. Gray’s will is the expression. Volitional Mending is one movement of
that expression.
Delegated Authority
Gray’s authority is delegated, not self-originated. This means Gray can act with great force, but his
ability to act is still dependent upon a higher permission.
Gray does not own reality. Gray does not replace God. Gray does not become the final center.
Gray is powerful because authority has been entrusted to him, not because he is independent from God.
This keeps Gray from becoming an idol within the lore. He is not worshiped. He is not ultimate. He is
not the source of all things. He is a being of extraordinary will operating inside a reality that still
belongs to God.

The Law of Subordinate Power
All power beneath God is subordinate power. Subordinate power may be vast, terrifying, beautiful,
incomprehensible, or nearly limitless from a creaturely perspective, but it is still not absolute. This
includes:
•
Gray’s OmniRender
•
Gray’s Volitional Mending
•
Gray’s Gestureless Agency
•
The Incomprehensibles
•
Omnimatter
•
Abstract forces
•
Dimensional laws
•
Reality manipulation
•
Conceptual authority
•
Memory-based realms
•
Possibility structures
•
Void entities
•
Paradox beings
All of these may function, but none of them are final. They are real, but they are not sovereign.
God as the Boundary of All Possibility
Possibility does not exist above God. Possibility exists beneath God. This is important because
Abstractspadium deals heavily with possibility, thought, imagination, memory, and conceptual worlds.
But possibility cannot become a second god. Possibility is not infinite in a way that surpasses God.
Possibility is only as wide as God permits it to be.
Possibility is not the highest realm. God is higher than possibility. This means even infinite possibility
has a boundary: God’s will.
Gray’s Role
Gray is not the sovereign center. Gray is a steward of Abstractspadium. Gray may preserve, defend,
render, restore, and interpret reality, but he does not possess final ownership over it.
Gray’s purpose is not domination. Gray’s purpose is preservation. Gray does not seek worship. Gray
seeks relationship, alignment, and participation. Gray’s highest wisdom is knowing that his will must

Volitional Mending Within This System
Volitional Mending is not sovereign creation. It is not Gray replacing God. It is not ultimate divine
authority. Volitional Mending is the authority of will to establish wholeness within the boundaries God
permits.
Gray does not mend because he is the final source of wholeness. Gray mends because his will has been
entrusted with the capacity to declare wholeness.
Gray can declare wholeness, but God defines wholeness.
That sentence protects the entire metaphysics. Gray may restore a thing, but God determines what
restoration truly is. Gray may declare integrity, but God determines the truth of integrity. Gray may
impose order, but God remains the source of order.
The Hierarchy of Reality
The metaphysical hierarchy of Abstractspadium is:
1. God — Absolute Sovereignty. The source, center, judge, sustainer, and final authority over all
things.
2. Divine Permission — The boundary by which anything may exist, act, continue, or be restrained.
3. Created Reality — All realms, dimensions, worlds, minds, memories, laws, systems, bodies, and
spaces.
4. Delegated Beings — Gray, Incomprehensibles, Paradoxi, abstract entities, and other beings
operating within permitted authority.
5. Operative Will — The ability of a being to act, declare, manifest, mend, resist, or preserve within its
granted domain.
6. Manifested Effect — The visible or invisible result of will acting inside permitted reality.
The Metaphysical Formula
For humans: Desire → Gesture → Process → Effect
For Gray: Will → Declaration → Effect
For God: Sovereignty → Reality

The Necessary Distinction

God alone possesses Absolute Sovereignty. Gray possesses extraordinary will, but not ultimate

In Abstractspadium, all things may be vast, abstract, impossible, infinite, or incomprehensible, but none
are above God. God remains the center. God remains the source. God remains the final authority. God

Abstractspadium Metaphysics  ·  7
Revised Signature Statement
Volitional Mending is the authority of will to declare wholeness such that reality itself conforms to that
declaration. It neither heals nor repairs. It establishes the complete state as the governing truth, making
restoration the immediate consequence of will rather than the result of process.
Theological Foundation
Within Abstractspadium, will is the operative force, not ultimate sovereignty.
Gray exercises extraordinary authority through will, but that authority is never independent of the One
from whom all authority ultimately proceeds. God alone remains absolutely sovereign over all
existence, all realities, all dimensions, and every expression of Abstractspadium.
Gray’s will does not rival God’s sovereignty. It operates beneath it.
Thus, Volitional Mending is not the power to become sovereign over reality, but the capacity to
establish wholeness through will within the authority that has been entrusted to Gray.
This preserves a meaningful hierarchy:
•
God → Absolute Sovereignty (the source of all authority and existence).
•
Gray → Operative Will (the capacity to act with entrusted authority).
•
Volitional Mending → One expression of that will, establishing wholeness without process.
That distinction allows Gray to remain immensely powerful while preserving the theological
foundation that Jesus Christ alone is Lord and ultimately sovereign.
